Questions Parents Ask

What kind of student is McSpadden Math Lab for?

The Math Lab is built for students who need more than worksheets and memorization. It’s for kids who are capable of deep thinking but aren’t getting the chance to explore, question, or make meaningful connections. Many join because they’re bored, checked out, or just “doing school.” They stay because they rediscover curiosity — and begin seeing math as something worth exploring.

How is this different from tutoring or learning centers?

Traditional tutoring fixes homework. Centers drill skills. McSpadden Math Lab develops enduring understandings. Students learn to reason, explore patterns, justify their decisions, and think flexibly from multiple angles. They move from passive learners to active mathematicians — engaged, curious, and confident.

What changes will I actually notice in my child?

Parents usually see a few things very quickly:

• They shift from bored to curious. • They begin exploring ideas instead of waiting for answers. • They start checking their thinking because they want to understand — not just finish. • They take on challenges they used to avoid.

In short, they transform from “getting through math” to actually thinking in math.

What if we have to miss a Saturday?

No problem. Exploration doesn’t hinge on perfect attendance. Because lessons build conceptual understanding — not isolated worksheets — your child stays connected to the big ideas even if they miss a week. I ensure they know the central strategy or concept the group built that day so they re-enter confidently.

My child is already advanced. Is this still valuable?

Absolutely. Advanced students often memorize quickly but haven’t developed the deep structures of mathematical thinking — flexible reasoning, patterns, conjecture, justification. Math Lab pushes them beyond “fast and correct” into meaningful exploration, challenge, and mathematical maturity.

How big are the groups?

Groups stay intentionally small so students can talk, explore ideas out loud, and build deep understanding through discussion and reasoning. Every child contributes. Every child gets challenged. This is not a sit-and-watch environment — it’s active mathematical exploration.

Do you offer a guarantee?

Yes. If your child does not show increased curiosity, engagement, or confidence in the first month, I’ll meet with them 1:1 to identify the barriers and help them reconnect with the joy of deep mathematical thinking. My goal is simple: every child should leave believing math is worth exploring.
