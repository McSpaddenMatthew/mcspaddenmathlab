How it works

We don’t tutor. We train thinkers.

Math Lab is not about racing ahead in the curriculum. It’s about building kids who can handle real thinking — the kind they’ll see in upper grades, advanced courses, and life.

Students wrestle with unfamiliar problems, compare strategies, and learn to stay calm under challenge. We use strategic games and puzzles (like Genius Star, Math Dice, and spatial challenges) because they build pattern recognition, logical sequencing, and cognitive endurance. There are no worksheets, homework packets, or test-prep tricks.

Clarity first

What Math Lab is — and what it isn’t.

Parents don’t need more mystery. Here’s exactly what we do and don’t do.

Is this tutoring?
No. We don’t sit next to your child and walk them through tonight’s homework. Math Lab is a training environment where capable kids get steady challenge, extension, and coaching in flexibility, efficiency, and problem solving.
Do you help with school homework or tests?
Our focus is on long-term skills: problem-solving, number sense, fractions, and confidence with hard questions. Those skills make homework and tests easier, but we do not use session time for school assignments.
Is this for kids who are behind?
Math Lab is designed for students who are at or above grade level and need to be stretched. If your child is significantly behind and needs one-on-one support, traditional tutoring will serve them better right now.
What if my child is nervous about being wrong?
That’s exactly the type of student we help. We treat mistakes as information, not something to be ashamed of. Over time, many kids go from “I hate being wrong” to “Let me try again a different way” and see errors as part of learning.
Who leads the sessions?
All labs are led by Mr. McSpadden, an experienced classroom teacher who specializes in helping elementary students grow in both skill and confidence. Your child will not be handed off to a rotating cast of tutors.
